Inclusivity Tool


Select a course from the drop-down menu to view inclusivity bugs based on Cognitive Styles:


On choosing a course, this tool checks for three decision rules, as detailed below:

  • Supporting learning by process using sub-instructions

    While brief instructions may suit some learners like Tim, who are comfortable with tinkering and exploring independently, the inclusion of subinstructions supports learners like Abi, who prefer a step-by-step learning approach.

  • Clear purpose behind tasks

    Abi, who prioritizes tasks aligned with their interests and relies on technologies, may be disproportionately affected by tasks lacking clear purposes. Explicit purpose behind tasks enables learners like Abi to assess the task's relevance and decide on its usefulness.

  • Drawing on prior knowledge for task guidance

    Linking tasks to prior knowledge empowers Abi by providing a structured approach to complete the task, utilizing familiar step-by-step processes from tutorials, how-to videos, and wizards.

  • Information link navigation

    Users like Abi click on a link only after gathering enough information and planning their next step. Labeled and descriptive links provide Abi with information about the webpage they are supposed to visit.
